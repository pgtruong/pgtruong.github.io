About Me

My passion for games began when my brother had introduced me to many of the technologies that are now considered retro. I had the luxury of being able to play the old Super Nintendo and Nintendo 64 games. As time went on, I had more access to other consoles and handhelds such as my Gameboy Advance and eventually a Windows XP PC. Once my brother bought a computer, he allowed me to have some time to play his games. I fell in love when I started playing Police Quest SWAT and Warcraft III. This brief glimpse in my childhood is what brought me to my passion for games today.

During my years in high school, I had no idea what I wanted to do except that I loved video games. Once senior year hit, I began to consistently play League of Legends. I tried to learn about the mechanics, the item builds, the strategies, everything. That is when I realized that high school had drained me from doing what I love: being dedicated and involved to games. When college applications hit, I knew I had to choose a major related to games so that I could be involved in what I love. I luckily got accepted to UCI as a Computer Game Science major and have not regretted it since. I honestly did not know of the challenges ahead of me and that making games would be very difficult. I accepted this, however, because my drive to belong with games was enough. The idea that I could influence peoples' lives by making games is just amazing. Like the teams that made the games that influenced my life, I hope to do the same to the future generations.

Now I am currently in my fourth year at UCI and am working on new projects. I have two in progress at the moment. The first is my senior capstone project that is two quarters long. This game project is called "Runeforge" and is a shop management and minigame collection hybrid. It is being created in Unity using C# and currently has a working demo(email me if you would like to try it). My second game project which is still in its design phase is "Dreamless" (not final title) and is a 2D puzzle platformer also being made in Unity using C#. Dreamless is a fun project that I am doing with friends that I hope to fully complete with original assets by the end of summer.

I was also a Data Assistant at the Center for Educational Partnerships at UCI for 2 years in my Freshman and Sophomore year. During this time, I was able to learn how to use Microsoft Excel to manage data, organize charts, and create pivot tables to use and show to my coworkers. I also managed databases in Filemaker Pro with a server provided to me and managed that data, adding and removing entries as needed. I left during my Sophomore year because I wanted more experience programming and designing rather than just management. Now I am actively searching for a job in the industry.
